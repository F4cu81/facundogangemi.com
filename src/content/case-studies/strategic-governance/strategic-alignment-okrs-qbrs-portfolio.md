## Executive Summary

An enterprise transformation environment needed to connect strategic priorities with execution routines across business and technology teams.

The work focused on OKRs, QBRs, portfolio governance and project office practices. The goal was to create a clearer rhythm for decision-making, prioritization, performance review and execution visibility.

## Context

Large organizations often define strategic priorities but struggle to translate them into consistent execution systems.

Leadership cadences, portfolio decisions, financial planning and team delivery may operate in parallel. When these routines are not connected, teams lose clarity and leaders lose visibility.

The organization needed a governance model that linked strategy, portfolio and execution.

## Business Challenge

The business challenge was strategic fragmentation.

Priorities existed, but execution routines did not always make progress, dependencies, risks and decisions visible at the right level. Leaders needed better mechanisms to review outcomes, adjust priorities and align teams around measurable goals.

The organization required a cadence that could support both strategic control and adaptive execution.

## Strategic Intervention

Facundo supported the implementation and improvement of governance practices such as OKRs, Quarterly Business Reviews and portfolio management routines.

The intervention helped connect executive priorities with team-level delivery and portfolio visibility. It also strengthened the role of project office practices in coordinating initiatives, improving transparency and enabling better decisions.

## Execution Model

The execution model included:

- OKR implementation and alignment routines.
- QBR design and facilitation practices.
- Portfolio governance and prioritization mechanisms.
- Project office operating routines.
- Jira, Microsoft Project, Confluence and collaboration practices where relevant.
- Leadership review cadences.
- Visibility over risks, dependencies and progress.
- Connection between strategic objectives and delivery execution.

## Business Impact

The organization gained a clearer governance rhythm and improved alignment between strategic priorities and execution routines.

Leadership improved its ability to review progress, identify risks, discuss priorities and connect transformation goals with measurable outcomes. Teams gained stronger context around how their work contributed to broader strategic objectives.

The impact is qualitative until specific metrics are validated for publication.
