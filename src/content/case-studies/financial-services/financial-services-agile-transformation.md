## Executive Summary

Several financial services environments needed to evolve from fragmented delivery practices toward more adaptive and coordinated ways of working.

The transformation focused on introducing agile delivery routines, clarifying roles, improving backlog discipline and connecting business priorities with technology execution. The work helped create repeatable patterns for agile adoption in complex financial contexts.

## Context

Financial services organizations often operate with strong regulatory pressure, multiple stakeholders, legacy processes and high coordination costs between business and technology.

In this context, delivery teams needed more than agile terminology. They needed a practical operating rhythm that improved visibility, decision-making and collaboration.

## Business Challenge

The organizations faced common transformation symptoms:

- Delivery visibility varied by team.
- Business and technology alignment depended too much on individual coordination.
- Teams lacked consistent delivery routines.
- Priorities were not always translated into clear backlogs.
- Decision cycles slowed execution.
- Agile adoption needed to work in real enterprise conditions, not only in isolated teams.

The challenge was to build a delivery model that teams could actually use and leaders could trust.

## Strategic Intervention

Facundo supported agile transformation through a combination of coaching, facilitation and operating model design.

The intervention included Scrum implementation, stakeholder alignment, Product Owner and Scrum Master enablement, backlog practices, team ceremonies, delivery metrics and scaling practices where needed.

In larger environments, the work also connected team-level execution with enterprise coordination mechanisms such as SAFe, Agile Release Trains, PI Planning and cross-team synchronization.

## Execution Model

The execution model included:

- Scrum adoption and team coaching.
- Product Owner and Scrum Master role clarification.
- Backlog organization and prioritization practices.
- Sprint ceremonies and delivery cadence.
- Cross-team coordination routines.
- SAFe practices for larger delivery environments.
- Leadership visibility over delivery progress.
- Continuous improvement through retrospectives and feedback loops.

## Business Impact

The transformation improved delivery visibility and created stronger coordination between business and technology teams.

The organizations gained more consistent delivery routines, clearer ownership and a repeatable model for broader agile adoption. Teams also improved their ability to plan, inspect progress and adapt based on feedback.
