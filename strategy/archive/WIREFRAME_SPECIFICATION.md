# WIREFRAME SPECIFICATION — facundogangemi.com

## Document Purpose

This document translates the homepage UX architecture into a practical wireframe specification.

It is intended to guide the future UI design and frontend development of facundogangemi.com.

The wireframe must preserve the strategic direction defined in:

- MASTER_CONTEXT.md
- BRAND_GUIDELINES.md
- CONTENT_BRIEFS.md
- HOMEPAGE_UX_ARCHITECTURE.md

The homepage must feel like:

> Executive AI Transformation Advisory

The wireframe must prioritize:

- executive authority
- premium perception
- AI Strategy positioning
- Enterprise Transformation credibility
- clear conversion flow
- fast comprehension
- strong visual hierarchy
- static-site scalability

---

# 1. Wireframe Design Principles

## 1.1 Primary UX Goal

The homepage must help qualified visitors understand Facundo's positioning in under 8 seconds.

The first viewport must answer:

1. Who is this person?
2. What does he help organizations do?
3. Why should I trust him?
4. What action should I take next?

---

## 1.2 Visual Hierarchy Principle

Every section must have a clear hierarchy:

1. Eyebrow / category label
2. Headline
3. Short explanatory text
4. Visual or structural proof
5. CTA

Do not create sections where all elements compete equally.

---

## 1.3 Conversion Principle

The page must follow a clear conversion ladder:

1. High intent: Book Advisory Call
2. Mid intent: Take AI Maturity Assessment
3. Low intent: Subscribe to Executive Briefing
4. Exploration intent: Explore Services / View Case Studies / Read Insights

Primary conversion must remain visible throughout the experience.

---

## 1.4 Layout Rhythm Principle

The homepage should alternate visual density:

- high impact hero
- compact trust bar
- spacious service explanation
- focused KPI band
- strategic framework section
- proof cards
- editorial insight section
- human layer
- lead magnet
- final CTA

This creates a premium editorial rhythm and prevents the site from feeling like a generic landing page.

---

# 2. Global Page Grid

## 2.1 Desktop Grid

Recommended desktop container:

- Max width: 1200px to 1280px
- Horizontal padding: 32px to 48px
- Grid: 12 columns
- Column gap: 24px
- Section vertical padding: 96px to 128px

Recommended breakpoints:

- Large desktop: 1280px+
- Desktop: 1024px to 1279px
- Tablet: 768px to 1023px
- Mobile: 360px to 767px

---

## 2.2 Mobile Grid

Recommended mobile layout:

- Single-column flow
- Horizontal padding: 20px to 24px
- Section vertical padding: 56px to 72px
- CTA buttons full width when needed
- Cards stacked vertically
- Diagrams simplified into vertical steps

---

## 2.3 Section Height Guidance

Hero:

- Desktop: 760px to 900px or 90vh
- Tablet: 680px to 760px
- Mobile: content-driven, approximately 640px to 760px

Trust bar:

- Desktop: 120px to 160px
- Mobile: 160px to 220px depending on logo layout

Standard sections:

- Desktop: 520px to 760px depending on content
- Mobile: content-driven

CTA bands:

- Desktop: 320px to 420px
- Mobile: 360px to 460px

# 3. Header / Navigation Wireframe

## 3.1 Desktop Header

### Layout

Full-width sticky header.

Structure:

- Left: logo / wordmark
- Center: navigation links
- Right: primary CTA button

Recommended navigation:

- Advisory
- Case Studies
- Insights
- Speaking
- About
- Contact

Primary CTA:

- Book Advisory Call

### Behavior

Initial state:

- Transparent or deep navy background
- Height: 72px
- White text
- CTA outlined or solid accent depending on hero contrast

Scrolled state:

- Glass blur or solid dark navy
- Height reduces to 64px
- Subtle bottom border
- CTA remains visible

### Visual Weight

Medium. Navigation must support the hero but not dominate it.

---

## 3.2 Mobile Header

### Layout

- Left: logo
- Right: hamburger icon
- Optional compact CTA icon or text link if space allows

### Mobile Menu

Full-screen or slide-down menu.

Menu structure:

1. Advisory
2. Case Studies
3. Insights
4. Speaking
5. About
6. Contact
7. Book Advisory Call button

### Behavior

- Header sticky on scroll
- Menu closes after selection
- CTA clearly separated from links

# Section 1 — Hero

## 4.1 Purpose

Establish positioning, authority and conversion intent immediately.

## 4.2 Desktop Wireframe

### Layout

Two-column hero.

Left column: 6 columns

- Eyebrow
- H1
- Subheadline
- CTA row
- Optional micro credibility line

Right column: 6 columns

- Executive portrait
- Abstract AI-inspired background

### Desktop Composition

Recommended arrangement:

- Hero content aligned vertically center.
- Portrait slightly overlaps background visual.
- Pull quote card appears to the right or lower-right of portrait.
- CTAs placed directly below subheadline.

### Content Order

1. Eyebrow
2. H1
3. Subheadline
4. Primary and secondary CTAs
5. Optional quote or credibility microcopy
6. Portrait visual

### Approximate Viewport Behavior

Above the fold should include:

- full H1
- subheadline
- both CTAs
- portrait
- part of trust transition or visual cue downward

## 4.3 Mobile Wireframe

### Layout

Single-column.

Order:

1. Eyebrow
2. H1
3. Subheadline
4. CTAs stacked
5. Portrait or cropped visual
6. Quote chip

### Mobile CTA Behavior

- Primary CTA full width.
- Secondary CTA full width or text link.
- Keep CTAs visible without excessive scrolling.

## 4.4 Visual Weight

Very high.

## 4.5 Interaction Zones

- Primary CTA
- Secondary CTA
- Quote chip hover not necessary
- Portrait background motion optional

## 4.6 Wireframe Notes

Avoid making the hero too busy.

The executive portrait must be the main visual anchor. AI visuals must support the positioning, not overpower the person.

# 7. Interaction Specification

## 7.1 Global Interactions

Recommended:

- Button hover: subtle lift or glow
- Card hover: 4px lift
- Link hover: underline or arrow shift
- Nav scroll: glass blur transition
- Section reveal: fade-up, staggered

Avoid:

- complex cursor effects
- excessive parallax
- heavy 3D animation
- scroll hijacking

---

## 7.2 Motion Timing

Recommended timings:

- Button hover: 150ms to 200ms
- Card hover: 200ms to 250ms
- Section fade-up: 500ms to 700ms
- Stagger delay: 80ms to 140ms
- KPI count-up: maximum 1200ms
- SVG line draw: 600ms to 900ms

# 10. Pre-UI Approval Checklist

Before moving into visual UI design, validate:

- Does the hero communicate executive AI transformation clearly?
- Is the first viewport premium and not crowded?
- Are trust signals visible early?
- Are services clear without becoming generic?
- Does the framework section create differentiation?
- Do case studies prove execution?
- Does Insights feel like executive thought leadership?
- Is the About section human but not CV-like?
- Is the AI Maturity Assessment positioned as premium?
- Is the CTA ladder clear?
- Does mobile remain clean and fast?
- Can the wireframe be built cleanly as a static site?
