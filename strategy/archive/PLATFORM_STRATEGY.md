## 4. Section Hierarchy

### Visual hierarchy rules
- **H1** (Manrope, 56–72px): one per page, strategic statement.
- **H2** (Manrope, 36–44px): section openers, no decorative styling.
- **H3** (Inter SemiBold, 22–26px): card titles, sub-sections.
- **Eyebrow label** (Inter, 12–13px, uppercase, +letter-spacing, orange): section context above H2.
- **Body** (Inter, 16–18px, line-height 1.6): max 65ch width.

### Spacing rhythm
- Section vertical rhythm: 96px desktop / 64px tablet / 48px mobile.
- 12-column grid, 1280px max-width, 24px gutters.
- Generous negative space — premium = breathing room.

### Section pattern (every page)
1. Eyebrow → 2. H2 → 3. 1-line strategic sub → 4. Content block → 5. Inline CTA.

## 5. CTA Strategy

### Three-tier CTA system

**Primary CTA — "Book an Advisory Call"**
- Style: solid orange `#F57C00`, white text, 8px radius, soft shadow, hover lift.
- Use: hero, closing band, sticky mobile bar, contact triggers.
- Goal: high-intent conversion (consulting opportunity).

**Secondary CTA — "Explore Services" / "Read Case Study"**
- Style: navy outline, transparent fill, hover fill orange.
- Use: mid-page exploration, alternative paths.
- Goal: nurture, discovery.

**Tertiary CTA — Inline arrow links** `Learn more →`
- Style: text-only, orange on hover, animated arrow.
- Use: cards, small modules.
- Goal: micro-engagement.

### Lead-capture ladder (low → high commitment)
1. Newsletter subscription (Executive Briefing).
2. AI Maturity Assessment (5-min interactive, gated PDF report).
3. Framework download (Intelligent Operating Model whitepaper).
4. Workshop / Keynote inquiry form.
5. Advisory discovery call (Calendly-style booking).

### CTA placement matrix
| Page | Hero | Mid | Close | Sticky |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Home | Book call + Explore | Assessment | Book call | Yes |
| Advisory hub | Book call | Framework | Book call | Yes |
| Service detail | Book call | Case study | Book call | Yes |
| Case study | — | Related | Book call | Yes |
| Insights article | Newsletter | Assessment | Related + Newsletter | No |
| About | Book call | Speaking | Book call | Yes |

### Anti-patterns to avoid
- Multiple competing primary CTAs in same view.
- Generic "Contact Us" — replace with intent-specific verbs.
- Pop-ups on landing — use scroll-triggered, dismissible cards instead.

## 7. User Experience Direction

### UX principles
- **Executive efficiency:** every page answers *"why does this matter to me, now?"* above the fold.
- **Scannable depth:** layered content — skim layer (headlines, bullets, KPIs) + deep layer (paragraphs, frameworks).
- **Trust by structure:** consistent rhythm, no surprises, no pop-up friction.
- **Premium calm:** dark surfaces, subtle motion, no neon, no parallax overload.

### Interaction model
- Sticky top nav with subtle blur + background fade after 80px scroll.
- Anchor-based mega-menu (no full-page reload between advisory subpages).
- Hover micro-interactions: 200ms ease-out, 2–4px translate, color shift to orange.
- Section reveals: fade + 8px translate on viewport intersection.
- Cursor: default — no custom cursor (executive, not creative-studio).
- No autoplay video. Hero motion = optional ambient particle layer, pausable, < 60kb.

### Performance budget
- LCP < 2.0s, CLS < 0.05, TBT < 150ms (target).
- Hero image: AVIF/WebP, < 120kb.
- Total homepage weight: < 1.2MB.
- Fonts: self-hosted Inter + Manrope, font-display: swap, max 2 weights each.

### Accessibility
- WCAG 2.2 AA minimum. AAA for body text contrast.
- Full keyboard navigation, visible focus rings (orange, 2px).
- Reduced-motion variants for all animations.
- Alt text on all imagery, structured headings, semantic landmarks.

### Responsive breakpoints
- Mobile: 360–767px (stacked, sticky bottom CTA).
- Tablet: 768–1023px (hybrid 2-col).
- Desktop: 1024–1439px (full grid).
- Wide: 1440px+ (max-width 1280, generous side margin).

### Trust micro-elements
- Inline credential chips (MIT, Management 3.0).
- Subtle testimonial quotes inside service pages.
- Logo bar in monochrome (never colorful — communicates restraint).
- "Last updated" stamps on Insights for freshness signal.

### Continuity rules
- Preserve all existing inbound link equity via 301 maps.
- Maintain RSS feed for Insights (legacy subscribers).

## 10. Strategic Messaging Direction

### Positioning statement (internal compass)
> *Facundo Gangemi helps executive teams transform their organizations into intelligent, AI-enabled operating models that deliver measurable business impact — combining 20+ years of enterprise experience with modern AI strategy.*

### Tagline candidates (rank-ordered)
1. **"Transforming ambition into results."** *(matches reference banner — recommended primary)*
2. "Where AI strategy meets enterprise execution."
3. "Intelligent transformation for modern organizations."
4. "AI strategy. Operating models. Real impact."

### Hero headline (recommended)
**"I help organizations transform with AI, agility and operating models that drive real results."**
*Sub:* Executive advisory for AI strategy, digital transformation and intelligent operating models.

### Messaging pillars (reuse across site)

**Pillar 1 — AI Strategy that creates business impact**
- Voice: pragmatic, anti-hype.
- Key lines: *"AI is not a tool — it's an operating model decision."* · *"From AI experiments to AI-enabled organizations."*

**Pillar 2 — Digital Transformation, executed**
- Voice: senior, executional.
- Key lines: *"Strategy without execution is theatre."* · *"Operating models that scale beyond the pilot."*

**Pillar 3 — Enterprise Agility, evolved**
- Voice: matured, beyond-Scrum.
- Key lines: *"Agility is an operating model, not a methodology."* · *"From Scrum ceremonies to enterprise outcomes."*

**Pillar 4 — Executive Enablement**
- Voice: peer-to-peer, advisory.
- Key lines: *"Equipping leaders to lead transformation."* · *"AI fluency at the executive table."*

### Vocabulary — adopt vs. avoid
**Adopt:** transformation, operating model, intelligent organization, executive advisory, business outcome, adoption, enablement, strategic execution, AI-enabled, modern enterprise.

**Avoid:** Scrum Master, Agile Coach (as primary identifier), ceremonies, sprints (in marketing copy), "passionate", "rockstar", "ninja", "10x", "disruption", "synergy", "AI-powered" (overused), generic "innovation".

### Tone calibration
- **Confident, not boastful.** Numbers and named clients > adjectives.
- **Strategic, not academic.** Short executive sentences > long theoretical paragraphs.
- **Human, not corporate.** First-person on About / hero; third-person on services.
- **Anti-hype.** Position AI as enterprise discipline, not magic.

### Proof architecture (use across all pages)
- **Named credibility:** Santander, BBVA, CaixaBank, Kruger, Mercantil Andina, Telefónica.
- **Institutional credibility:** MIT Digital Transformation, Management 3.0, Founder Berater Digital + Lab.
- **Quantified credibility:** 20+ years · 50+ engagements · 10+ industries.
- **Editorial credibility:** Insights frequency + LinkedIn presence.
- **Speaking credibility:** stages, panels, keynotes (build out).
