# HOMEPAGE UX ARCHITECTURE — facundogangemi.com

## Document Purpose

This document defines the homepage user experience architecture for facundogangemi.com.

It translates the strategic positioning, brand guidelines and content briefs into a practical UX structure that can later guide wireframes, UI design and frontend development.

The homepage must work as:

- an executive authority platform
- a premium personal brand experience
- a consulting conversion system
- a thought leadership gateway
- a bridge between AI Strategy, Digital Transformation and Enterprise Transformation

The homepage must not feel like:

- a freelancer portfolio
- a generic consulting website
- a traditional Agile Coach website
- a WordPress-style blog homepage
- a startup landing page with excessive hype

---

# 1. Homepage Strategic Objective

The homepage must establish authority and conversion intent within the first scroll session.

Within the first 8 seconds, visitors should understand:

1. Who Facundo Gangemi is.
2. What type of transformation he helps organizations lead.
3. Who he works with.
4. Why his positioning is different.
5. What action they should take next.

The homepage must position Facundo as:

- AI Strategy Advisor
- Digital Transformation Executive
- Enterprise Transformation Consultant
- Executive AI Transformation Leader
- Intelligent Operating Model Strategist

The homepage must evolve perception away from:

- Agile Coach
- Scrum trainer
- general digital coach
- methodology consultant

---

The homepage experience should feel like:

> Executive AI Transformation Advisory

The emotional experience must be:

- calm

# 4. Recommended Homepage Section Flow

## Section 1 — Hero

### Objective

Capture executive authority immediately and communicate the new positioning clearly.

### UX Role

The hero is the authority anchor. It must make the visitor understand that Facundo is not only a coach or trainer, but an executive advisor for AI-era transformation.

### Recommended Layout

Desktop:

- Left side: strategic headline, subheadline, CTAs and optional credibility microcopy.
- Right side: executive portrait with subtle AI-inspired background.
- Background: deep navy with subtle radial gradient or abstract AI particle field.

Mobile:

- Headline first.
- Portrait second or background treatment.
- CTAs stacked.
- Trust signal appears quickly after CTAs.

### Recommended Content

Eyebrow:

AI Strategy · Digital Transformation · Enterprise Impact

Primary H1 option:

AI Strategy & Enterprise Transformation for organizations that need real execution.

Alternative H1 option:

I help organizations transform with AI, agility and operating models that drive real results.

Subheadline:

Executive advisory for AI strategy, digital transformation and intelligent operating models.

Strategic quote chip:

The future belongs to organizations that combine intelligence, adaptability and execution.

### CTA Strategy

Primary CTA:

- Book Advisory Call

Secondary CTA:

- Explore Services

Optional tertiary micro-link:

- View Case Studies

### Visual Weight

Very high. This is the most important section of the homepage.

### Motion Direction

- Fade-up headline reveal.
- Subheadline appears 120ms later.
- CTA staggered reveal.
- Portrait subtle parallax.
- Background particle field must be subtle and lightweight.
- Respect prefers-reduced-motion.

### UX Constraints

Avoid:

- too much text
- excessive AI hype
- cyberpunk visuals
- generic motivational statements
- startup-style exaggerated claims

# 5. Global Homepage UX Rules

## 5.1 CTA Hierarchy

The homepage must maintain a clear conversion ladder:

1. High intent: Book Advisory Call
2. Mid intent: Take AI Maturity Assessment
3. Low intent: Subscribe to Executive Briefing
4. Exploration: Explore Services / View Case Studies / Read Insights

Primary CTA must always be visually dominant.

Secondary CTAs must support exploration without competing with the primary action.

---

## 5.2 Navigation Behavior

Recommended header structure:

- Logo
- Advisory
- Case Studies
- Insights
- Speaking
- About
- Contact or Book Advisory Call

Preferred desktop behavior:

- Sticky navigation.
- Glass blur after 80px scroll.
- Height reduces from 72px to 64px.
- Primary CTA remains visible.

Preferred mobile behavior:

- Hamburger menu.
- Sticky top bar.
- Book Advisory Call remains prominent inside menu.

---

## 5.3 Visual Rhythm

The homepage should alternate visual intensity:

1. Dark premium hero
2. Dark trust bar
3. Light advisory grid
4. Dark KPI strip
5. Light or dark framework section depending on composition
6. Light case studies
7. Light insights
8. Dark about/speaking or alternating editorial blocks
9. Dark assessment block
10. Light newsletter
11. Dark final CTA

This rhythm avoids monotony and creates premium editorial pacing.

---

## 5.4 Spacing Guidelines

Recommended desktop rhythm:

- Section vertical padding: 96px to 128px.
- Hero minimum height: 90vh or 760px max visual height.
- Content max width: 1200px to 1280px.
- Reading width: 640px to 760px.
- Card grid gap: 24px to 32px.

Recommended mobile rhythm:

- Section padding: 56px to 72px.
- Hero should not exceed unnecessary scroll depth.
- CTA buttons should be large and thumb-friendly.

---

## 5.5 Typography Direction

Typography must feel:

- clean
- executive
- modern
- highly readable
- premium

Recommended direction:

- H1: large, calm, confident.
- H2: strategic, editorial.
- Body: concise and readable.
- Labels/eyebrows: uppercase, letter-spaced, accent color.

Avoid:

- exaggerated startup typography
- long paragraphs
- excessive bold text
- decorative fonts

---

## 5.6 Motion Principles

Motion must support perception, not decoration.

Recommended motion:

- fade-up reveals
- soft hover states
- subtle parallax
- count-up KPIs
- SVG line-draw for frameworks
- smooth sticky navigation transition

Avoid:

- flashy animations
- constant motion
- heavy particle systems
- interactions that reduce performance

Accessibility requirement:

- All motion must respect prefers-reduced-motion.

---

## 5.7 Mobile Experience Principles

Mobile must not feel like a compressed desktop site.

Priorities:

1. Clear headline.
2. Immediate CTA.
3. Fast access to trust signals.
4. Single-column service clarity.
5. Readable article cards.
6. Sticky or easily accessible CTA.

Mobile constraints:

- Avoid large heavy animations.
- Avoid complex diagrams without simplified mobile version.
- Avoid dense text blocks.

# 7. Static Website / GitHub Pages Considerations

The homepage should be designed for a static website architecture.

Recommended future technical direction:

- Static site compatible with GitHub Pages.
- Content-driven Insights section.
- Articles stored as Markdown with frontmatter.
- Case studies stored as Markdown or JSON.
- Reusable components.
- SEO metadata generated per page.
- Sitemap and RSS generated during build if using a static generator.

Important content system decision:

Make.com should not generate raw HTML articles directly.

Preferred future flow:

Make.com → Markdown with frontmatter → /content/insights → static site renders article pages and listing pages.

This keeps design, SEO and structure consistent.

---

# 8. Homepage Success Criteria

The homepage is successful if:

- The positioning shift is obvious within 8 seconds.
- The site feels premium and executive.
- AI is presented strategically, not as hype.
- Agile is reframed as enterprise transformation and operating model capability.
- The visitor can clearly understand the offer.
- Trust is established early.
- Case studies and insights support authority.
- The primary CTA is always clear.
- The experience feels fast, modern and credible.
- The homepage can scale into a full static site ecosystem.

---

# 9. Pre-Wireframe QA Checklist

Before creating wireframes, validate:

- Does the hero communicate AI Strategy and Enterprise Transformation clearly?
- Is the homepage free from Agile-only positioning?
- Does the first scroll contain authority, trust and offer clarity?
- Are Blog/Insights and Case Studies preserved but elevated?
- Is there a clear conversion ladder?
- Is the visual direction premium and not generic?
- Does the page feel executive, not freelancer-style?
- Are CTAs consistent across the homepage?
- Can this structure be implemented cleanly as a static site?
- Can the Insights system support future Make.com automation?
